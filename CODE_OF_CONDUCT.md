# Code of Conduct

## ü§ù Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in the OpsSight DevOps Visibility Platform community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## üìã Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment for our community include:

### ‚úÖ Encouraged Behaviors
- **Respectful Communication**: Using welcoming and inclusive language
- **Constructive Feedback**: Providing helpful, actionable feedback
- **Professional Conduct**: Maintaining professionalism in all interactions
- **Collaborative Spirit**: Working together towards common goals
- **Empathy and Understanding**: Showing empathy towards other community members
- **Learning Mindset**: Being open to learning and growing together
- **Recognition**: Giving credit where credit is due
- **Inclusivity**: Making everyone feel welcome regardless of background

### ‚ùå Unacceptable Behaviors
- **Harassment**: Trolling, insulting/derogatory comments, and personal attacks
- **Discrimination**: Discriminatory language or actions based on personal characteristics
- **Privacy Violations**: Publishing others' private information without permission
- **Inappropriate Content**: Sexual language, imagery, or advances of any kind
- **Spam and Off-topic**: Excessive promotional content or off-topic discussions
- **Disruptive Behavior**: Deliberately disrupting discussions or development work
- **Threats and Intimidation**: Any form of threats or intimidating behavior
- **Doxxing**: Sharing personal information without consent

## üõ°Ô∏è Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Community leaders have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned to this Code of Conduct, and will communicate reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate.

## üåç Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, and also applies when an individual is officially representing the community in public spaces. Examples of representing our community include using an official e-mail address, posting via an official social media account, or acting as an appointed representative at an online or offline event.

### Community Spaces Include:
- GitHub repository (issues, discussions, pull requests)
- Discord server
- Community forums
- Social media channels
- Conferences and meetups
- Any other forums created by the project team

## üìû Reporting Guidelines

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported to the community leaders responsible for enforcement.

### How to Report

#### üîí Private Reporting (Recommended)
- **Email**: conduct@opssight.dev
- **Discord**: Direct message to @moderators
- **GitHub**: Use the [Report Content](https://docs.github.com/en/communities/maintaining-your-safety-on-github/reporting-abuse-or-spam) feature

#### üìù What to Include
When reporting an incident, please include:
- **Description**: What happened
- **Context**: When and where it occurred
- **Evidence**: Links, screenshots, or other documentation
- **Impact**: How it affected you or others
- **Desired Outcome**: What you'd like to see happen

### üïê Response Timeline
- **Acknowledgment**: Within 24 hours
- **Investigation**: Within 72 hours
- **Resolution**: Within 7 days
- **Follow-up**: Within 14 days

All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly. All community leaders are obligated to respect the privacy and security of the reporter of any incident.

## ‚öñÔ∏è Enforcement Guidelines

Community leaders will follow these Community Impact Guidelines in determining the consequences for any action they deem in violation of this Code of Conduct:

### 1. üìù Correction
**Community Impact**: Use of inappropriate language or other behavior deemed unprofessional or unwelcome in the community.

**Consequence**: A private, written warning from community leaders, providing clarity around the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the behavior was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.

### 2. ‚ö†Ô∏è Warning
**Community Impact**: A violation through a single incident or series of actions.

**Consequence**: A warning with consequences for continued behavior. No interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, for a specified period of time. This includes avoiding interactions in community spaces as well as external channels like social media. Violating these terms may lead to a temporary or permanent ban.

### 3. ‚è∏Ô∏è Temporary Ban
**Community Impact**: A serious violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior.

**Consequence**: A temporary ban from any sort of interaction or public communication with the community for a specified period of time. No public or private interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, is allowed during this period. Violating these terms may lead to a permanent ban.

### 4. üö´ Permanent Ban
**Community Impact**: Demonstrating a pattern of violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior, harassment of an individual, or aggression toward or disparagement of classes of individuals.

**Consequence**: A permanent ban from any sort of public interaction within the community.

## üìñ Additional Guidelines

### Contributing Guidelines
- **Code Style**: Follow the established coding standards
- **Documentation**: Document your contributions clearly
- **Testing**: Include appropriate tests with your changes
- **Communication**: Use clear, descriptive commit messages and PR descriptions

### Discussion Guidelines
- **Stay On Topic**: Keep discussions relevant to the project
- **Search First**: Check if your question has been asked before
- **Be Specific**: Provide detailed information when asking for help
- **Help Others**: Share your knowledge to help fellow community members

- **Use Templates**: Fill out issue and PR templates completely
- **Be Patient**: Allow time for review and feedback
- **Be Responsive**: Respond to feedback and questions promptly
- **Follow Up**: Update issues and PRs with current status
